Product Design

SendIT App

End-to-end product design for a parcel delivery app — focused on making the booking flow feel instant, and tracking feel transparent and reassuring.

Role
Product Designer
Year
2023
Tools
Figma

Overview

The Challenge

Most parcel delivery apps feel like afterthoughts — clunky forms, unclear pricing, and anxiety-inducing tracking pages. SendIT set out to change that: a delivery app that actually respects your time and keeps you informed every step of the way.

My role was to design the full mobile experience, from onboarding through to delivery confirmation — covering booking, payment, tracking, and notifications.

Process

Design Process

01

Research

Competitive analysis of Uber, DHL, and Lalamove apps; identified key pain points in booking flows.

02

User Flows

Mapped the core journeys: send a parcel, track a delivery, manage account.

03

Prototyping

Iterative prototyping in Figma with progressive fidelity — quick to validate, quick to change.

04

UI Design

High-fidelity screens with a component library built for the engineering handoff.

Screens

App Screens

The interface prioritises speed — the most common action (book a delivery) is reachable in two taps from the home screen. Tracking uses a live map with clear status milestones rather than vague status text.

Prototype

Interaction Design

A key goal was making the app feel responsive and alive. Micro-animations on booking confirmation and delivery status transitions reduce perceived waiting time and build user trust.
